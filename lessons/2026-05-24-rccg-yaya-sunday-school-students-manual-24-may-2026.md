THE DANGERS INVOLVED

The Scripture mentions that this sexual pervasion will result in abandonment from God and the hardening of the perpetrator’s heart (Romans 1:28). God will give them over to continue in things that are ‘not convenient’ i.e. ‘not becoming’, ‘inappropriate’, ‘shameful’.

As a result, they can no longer see the effects of their wrongdoings (Proverbs 10:17; Proverbs 14:12). Without an awareness of their sinfulness, there will be no repentance (2 Corinthians 4:3-4). Without repentance, there will be no forgiveness (Acts 8:22). Without forgiveness, there is no sol vation (2 Corinthians 7:10; Romans 10:10).

CHRISTIANS’ DISPOSITION

Christians are called to demonstrate the love of Christ towards all, including homosexuals (Matthew 22:39). Homosexuality is a sin and like any other sin, it needs to be laid at the cross and repented of (Romans 6:6; Ephesians 4:22-24).

Christians should pray for the salvation of ho-mosexuals the same way they would any other person in sin (1 Timothy 2:1; James 5:16). They should treat homosexuals with the same dignity as they would anyone else because they are also made in the image of God (1 Peter 2:17a; Genesis 1:27). However, this does not mean that Christians should approve of their sin (Ephesians 5:11; 1 Timothy 5:22b).

Although, our society does not rely on the Bible for its moral truth but on humanistic and relativis-tic morals upon which it builds its ethical struc-ture, the Bible, of course, condemns homosexual-ity (Leviticus 18:22, 20:13). It takes no ‘leap of logic’ to discern that homosexual marriage is also condemned. Therefore, every Christian and the church at large must continue to speak the truth in love and pray for sinners until they come to the knowledge of Christ (Ephesians 4:15).

CONCLUSION: Let the love of Christ flow through you so that sinners can see true love and turn to Christ.
